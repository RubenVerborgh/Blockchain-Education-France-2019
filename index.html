Will blockchain reinvent
the Internet?

  • blockchain
    • without central mediator
    • parties want to reach a common agreement
    • while not trusting each other
  • Internet
    • without central mediator
    • parties want to disagree
    • and say their own things

The Solid ecosystem enables people to use the apps they need, while
storing their data wherever they want.

People own their data, and share it
with the apps and people they choose.

People choose where they store
every single piece of data they produce.

They can grant apps and people access
to very specific parts of their data.

Separating app and storage competition
drives permissionless innovation.

Solid is an ecosystem of data and apps
that work seamlessly together.

  • data pods
    • profile
    • photos
    • comments
    • likes
    • viewing statistics
    • …
  • applications
    • news reader
    • video player
    • document collaboration
    • …
  • standards
    • HTTP
    • URL
    • Resource Description Framework
    • Linked Data Platform
    • Linked Data Notifications
    • …

Will we lose access to customers’ data
we need for analytics?

  • No. Their version becomes the source of truth.
  • Media companies can still keep a copy,
    but don’t have to.
    • We can argue that it is our data too.
    • Storage might be expensive under GDPR.

Can we trust data that comes
from consumers?

  • No.
  • Yes.
    • The source can digitally sign the data.

What should we do if
we are not collecting data anymore?

  • Relax.
  • Focus on what you really want to do.
    • …which is most likely not data harvesting.

There will not be less data.
There will be more.

  • Not just the data you generate.
  • Data from many other sources.
